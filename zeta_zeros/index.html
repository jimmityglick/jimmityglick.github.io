The First Ten Non-Trivial Zeros

Each zero acts as a frequency in a harmonic series that reconstructs the distribution of primes. The first zero at t ≈ 14.13 is the "fundamental frequency" — the dominant correction to the prime counting estimate. Higher zeros add finer and finer detail, like overtones in a musical note. If even one of these zeros drifted off the line Re(s) = ½, it would introduce a rogue harmonic — a violent distortion in how primes are distributed.
